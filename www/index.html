Investigation of dependencies between multiple data sources allows the discovery of regularities and interactions that are not seen in individual data sets. The importance of such methods is increasing with the availability and size of co-occurring data sets in computational biology, open data initiatives, and other domains. Practical implementations of general-purpose models provide necessary tools for realizing the full potential of these information sources.

Your feedback and contributions are welcome. For further details, see the project page at R-Forge, or contact Leo Lahti.

Software & Documentation

Current version provides the following functionality in three independent modules (follow links for documentation and source code):
  • dependency-based dimensionality reduction and preprocessing (drCCA; Tripathi et al., 2008)
  • decompositions into shared and independent components (pint; Lahti et al., 2009)
  • multi-way modeling of co-occurrence data (multiWayCCA; Huopaniemi et al., 2010)

These will later be integrated to provide a unified access to various dependency modeling tools. See also our project page at R-Forge.

Background

Techniques for the discovery and analysis of statistical dependencies are implemented in R, an open source environment for statistical computing.

The implementations are based on well-established models such as probabilistic canonical correlation analysis and multi-task learning [1-5]. Probabilistic framework deals rigorously with the uncertainties associated with small sample sizes, and allows incorporation of prior information in the analysis.

The applicability of the models has been demonstrated in previous case studies in functional genomics [2-4]. The general modeling framework is not restricted to specific applications, however.
